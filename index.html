          Discussion

          1. Everyone knew the dividend odds, so everyone could compute the fundamental value. Why did trades still happen away from it?
          2. Look at who finished at the top. Did they trade close to fundamentals, or ride the momentum and get out in time?
          3. Watch the tape near the peak: who was still buying? What were they expecting?
          4. If we ran this exact market again with the same group, would the bubble be bigger or smaller? (Smith found bubbles shrink with experience.)
          5. What real-world markets does this remind you of — and what was that market's "final period"?
